Overview

This coffee table book delves into the philosophy of dressing with intention. It reimagines fashion as a lifestyle choice, offering readers an enriching journey through the origins of their clothing, the significance of ethical production, and the power of conscious consumption. Each chapter unravels a unique narrative, blending compelling stories, vibrant visuals, and actionable advice to inspire thoughtful wardrobe choices.

The book celebrates authenticity, creativity, and sustainability, guiding readers toward a deeper connection with their clothing. It serves as a roadmap to mindful dressing, empowering individuals to make informed decisions that reflect their values and positively impact the planet. By merging education with inspiration, the book becomes a catalyst for embracing meaningful change in fashion.

My Approach

Research: The rise of sustainable fashion has become a central focus within the industry, with brands increasingly adopting eco-responsible practices. While interconnected, slow fashion prioritizes intentional production, superior craftsmanship, and longevity, whereas sustainable fashion emphasizes minimizing environmental impact across a garment’s lifecycle. This research examined the adoption, advantages, and challenges of slow fashion, aiming to empower readers to embrace transformative change and make conscious, impactful choices.

Design: Extensive research informed a design that captured the essence of slow fashion through its visual and tactile elements. A minimalist design ensured clarity and engagement, with concise text and an uncluttered layout. For the tangible format, I selected sustainably produced, zero-waste stipple-textured paper (270 gsm) and paired it with a durable canvas cover. This combination reinforced the organic aesthetic, environmental responsibility, and enduring principles of slow fashion.

Reflections

This project offered valuable insights into bridging creativity with functionality. It reinforced the importance of:

Consumer-Centric Design: Solving real-world problems rather than solely focusing on aesthetics.

Feedback Integration: Iterative logo design through peer and mentor feedback ensured simplicity and recall value.

Challenges: Identifying a brand name and logo that balances uniqueness and memorability. Addressing scalability and sustainability while appealing to both fashion-forward and non-fashion-centric audiences.

Key Takeaways: Understanding market demands extends beyond design; it requires empathy, adaptability, and strategic thinking. Feedback loops and real-world applications are crucial to achieving a brand’s success.
